The National Picture

Australia's population has grown dramatically — but not evenly

Total population crossed 27 million, but the distribution of that growth tells a different story. Some cities absorbed almost all of it; large swathes of the country barely moved.

Population Growth Across Australia

Where People Are Concentrated

Bubble map · Absolute population by major city

Rapid growth in Western Australia and Queensland suggests Australia's population is spreading beyond the traditional east-coast centres. However, the bubble map shows Melbourne and Sydney still dominate in absolute population size, highlighting the difference between fast-growing regions and where most Australians currently live.

Growth Rate vs. Net Overseas Migration

Connected scatterplot · Each point is one year — the path shows how migration drove growth

While population growth was uneven across states, the national trend was heavily influenced by overseas migration. After border restrictions ended, net overseas migration more than doubled between 2022 and 2023, corresponding with a jump in population growth from 1.0% to 2.3%, the highest rate shown in the series.

02

The Migration Engine

Overseas migration is the primary driver — and it almost stopped

Net overseas migration has outpaced natural increase for most of the decade. The pandemic severed it almost entirely in 2020 — and the rebound that followed was historic.

Components of Population Change

Streamgraph · Natural increase vs. net overseas migration, 2015–2025

Who Is Arriving

Population pyramid · Age and sex of overseas migrants

The pandemic exposed how reliant Australia's growth is on migration. When overseas arrivals collapsed in 2021, migration briefly became negative and population growth stalled; when borders reopened, a surge of mostly 20–34-year-olds fuelled the strongest migration rebound in the series.

03

Where They Come From

A handful of countries account for the majority of arrivals

India has overtaken China and the UK as Australia's largest source of migrants. The shift is not just demographic — it reflects a decade of deliberate visa policy change.

Countries Gaining and Losing Share

Source Country Mix Over Time

Stacked normalised bar · Top source countries as % of annual arrivals

The composition of migration to Australia has changed significantly since 2015. The stacked bars show India's share rising to dominate recent arrivals, while the diverging bar highlights a sharp decline in China's contribution, reflecting a broader shift in Australia's migration patterns toward South Asian source countries.

Where Each Country's Migrants Settle

Heatmap · Migrant arrivals by country of origin and destination state — use the slider to explore each year

The shift toward South Asian migration has not fundamentally changed where migrants settle. Despite major changes in source countries over the decade, most arrivals continue to concentrate in New South Wales and Victoria, suggesting that existing employment opportunities, education hubs, and migrant communities remain powerful settlement magnets.

How Visa Policy Reshaped the Intake

Lollipop · Change in visa grants by category, 2015–2025

Australia's migration story over the past decade was shaped as much by policy as by demand. The sharp expansion of regional and nominated visa pathways, alongside a major reduction in Skilled Independent visas, helped redirect migrant flows and reshape the country's source-country profile.

04

The State Race

Not all states are keeping up

Victoria and Queensland have punched above their weight. South Australia and Tasmania have slipped. The rank changes over the decade tell the most dramatic version of the story.

The State Growth Rankings, Year by Year

Bump chart · Rank 1 = fastest growing — line crossings are the story

Growth Rate Shift, 2016 → 2025

Slope chart · How each state's annual growth rate changed over the decade

State growth patterns have diverged significantly over the past decade. Western Australia rose from one of the slower-growing states in 2016 to the fastest-growing by 2025, while Tasmania moved in the opposite direction, with the bump chart showing a sharp fall in ranking and the slope chart confirming a decline in growth rates.

Queensland's rise appears more sustained than cyclical. While several states experienced short-term fluctuations in their rankings, Queensland consistently remained near the top of the growth table and its trajectory shows steady population expansion throughout the decade.

Yet population size remains highly uneven. Even as Western Australia and Queensland accelerated, New South Wales and Victoria continue to contain the largest populations, as shown in the small-multiple trajectories below.
